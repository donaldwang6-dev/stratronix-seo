The EU AI Act (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689) is fully enforceable from August 2026. Private AI Appliances (PAA) give EU deployers one of the cleanest paths to compliance because model weights, prompts and completions all remain on the controller's premises. This guide walks through the Act's obligations in the order they bite at go-live, and shows where a PAA removes the heaviest compliance work.

High-risk obligations and how PAA delivers them

  1. Risk-management system across the lifecycle — STRATRONIX PAA logs every model and corpus version; risk assessments can be diff-tracked between versions.
  2. Data governance — training and fine-tuning data is curated on premises, with provenance recording baked into the appliance.
  3. Technical documentation (Annex IV) — auto-generated export covers design, model, intended purpose, risk analysis and deployment context.
  4. Record-keeping — automatic logs of events, prompts, completions, retrieval sources; retention up to 10 years for high-risk systems.
  5. Transparency & user information — appliance exposes AI-generated markers and disclosure banners configurable per workflow.
  6. Human oversight — PAA's confidence scores and human-in-the-loop gating make Art. 14 oversight verifiable.
  7. Accuracy, robustness, cybersecurity — appliance tracks drift, supports adversarial tests, and ships with hardware root-of-trust.
  8. Post-market monitoring — STRATRONIX PAA emits live KPI streams (accuracy, refusal rate, latency) to feed the deployer's post-market system.
Why PAA helps. Most of the Act's obligations are evidence duties. A PAA turns each duty into a structured log or export — instead of reconstructing paperwork after the fact.

CE marking and conformity assessment

For most high-risk AI systems, deployers can rely on the appliance vendor's conformity assessment if the vendor has documented the technical file and signed the EU declaration of conformity. STRATRONIX maintains a rolling CE file for the STA-100 PAA covering Annex IV; deployers append their configuration as a hosted environment addendum.

Post-market monitoring in practice

Under Art. 72 deployers must monitor real-world performance and report serious incidents. With PAA:

  • Live KPI streaming to SIEM / GRC platforms (accuracy, refusal rate, time-to-human-overturn).
  • Incident tickets auto-opened on threshold breach.
  • Annual post-market report auto-emitted with regulator-ready text.

Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment (FRIA)

Under Art. 27 deployers of certain high-risk AI systems (public services, credit, employment, law enforcement) must run a FRIA. A PAA makes the FRIA a structured exercise: the appliance exports the deployment context, affected groups, oversight measures and redress mechanisms.

Translating to member-state realities

National AI Act authorities open in 2026 (DE: BfDI & BNetzA; FR: CNIL & ARCEP; ES: AESIA & AEPD; IT: AgID & Garante; NL: AP; PL: UODO & UKE). PAA deployments feed their reporting tools natively because the evidence is already structured.

Sanction levels

  • Prohibited practice — up to €35M or 7% global turnover.
  • High-risk non-compliance — up to €15M or 3%.
  • Other obligations — up to €7.5M or 1%.

A PAA materially reduces the risk of every category by automating evidence production.

FAQ — EU AI Act Compliance with PAA

Q1. Is a PAA itself an AI system under the Act?
A. Not necessarily — the Act regulates the deployed AI use case. A PAA is the infrastructure; the workflow you run on it is the regulated system. STRATRONIX provides per-workflow classification.

Q2. Do I still need an external conformity assessment?
A. For most Annex III use cases, the appliance vendor's conformity assessment suffices when the deployer appends its configuration. STRATRONIX provides a conformity checklist.

Q3. How does PAA help with Art. 14 human oversight?
A. The appliance exposes confidence scores, retrieval sources, and configurable human-in-the-loop gating; oversight events are logged.

Q4. What about Art. 50 transparency for chatbots?
A. Limited-risk workflows can prepend a configurable "You are talking to an AI" disclosure on every appliance-generated reply.

Q5. What changes in 2027 and 2028?
A. Most high-risk obligations are live in 2026; GPAI obligations phase in Aug 2025/2027. STRATRONIX maintains a regulatory timeline accessible from the appliance admin UI.
